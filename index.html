The situation

Security pressure usually arrives before a security team does.

Most small businesses don't decide to invest in security — they get asked to prove it, usually on a deadline. Any of this sound familiar?

  • A customer asks for SOC 2 before renewing a contract.
  • An insurer asks about MFA, backups, EDR, and incident response — and the answers aren't clear.
  • A regulated client needs HIPAA or GLBA documentation you don't have yet.
  • A vendor security questionnaire arrives, and nobody is quite sure how to answer it.
  • Policies exist somewhere, but they don't match how the business actually operates.
  • Microsoft or Google environments have grown messy, with no one reviewing access or configuration.
  • Contractors and staff use personal devices and accounts for company work.
  • Leadership needs a plain-English roadmap, not a 40-page framework document.

Services

Practical support, scoped to what you actually need.

Each engagement is advisory in nature — built to help you understand requirements, close real gaps, and communicate clearly, not to replace legal, audit, or certification bodies.

Compliance Gap Review

Vendor & Cyber Insurance Questionnaire Support

Microsoft 365 Security Baseline Review

Review of MFA, admin roles, access controls, Intune/Defender posture, logging, device controls, and practical security settings sized for small teams.

Policy, Evidence & Roadmap Support

AI-accelerated drafting and organization with human review, so policies, evidence, and controls actually match how the business operates.

How engagements work

A straightforward four-step process.

  1. 01

    Discover

    Understand the business model, data, systems, vendors, and what's driving the compliance pressure in the first place.

  2. 02

    Map

    Identify the relevant frameworks, controls, risks, and evidence needed to respond credibly.

  3. 03

    Prioritize

    Separate urgent security gaps from low-value compliance theater, so effort goes where it matters.

  4. 04

    Deliver

    Provide a clear roadmap, policy and evidence guidance, and practical next-step recommendations.

About

Built for practical security, not security theater.

Sentinel Keep Cyber Advisory is led by Jonathan Rothe, an IT and cybersecurity professional with years of hands-on experience in the tech industry — supporting SaaS platforms at scale, troubleshooting API integrations, and investigating issues through Splunk and audit-log analysis.

That background extends into security operations and compliance: SOC analyst work, escalating real incidents found in production logs, building HIPAA and SOC 2 readiness programs from near zero (policies, risk registers, tabletop exercises), and hands-on work in Microsoft cloud environments including Entra, Intune, and Defender.

The focus is simple: help small teams understand what actually matters, document what is true about their environment, and build security programs they can realistically operate — not ones that only look good in a binder.

How AI fits in

AI-assisted, human-reviewed.

AI can accelerate policy drafting, research, and documentation — but it cannot replace security judgment.
